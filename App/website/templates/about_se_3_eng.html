SPECIAL EXAM 3 (HIDE AND SEEK FRIENDSHIP)

üí•Content:

  • 6 contestants are divided into 2 groups to play hide and seek on an island, each group selects 1 person to hide and the rest will search for the hider of the opposing team.
  • When the exam starts, the hider of each side chooses a partial square to hide in and will not be allowed to move during the exam, then the seekers choose a partial square they want to start.
  • The seekers are allowed to move or stand still at any time. In addition, the seeker can search a partial square to see if there is a hider from the opposing team there. Each seeker will have only 1 turn to search during the exam.
  • The hider has the option to surrender. When chosen, that group will lose and the seekers will lose 10pt each while the hider will lose nothing.

ü¶ÑMechanism:

  • The contestants on the island will continuously lose water over time, and when the water bar drops to 0, that contestant will be eliminated. (If the hider is eliminated due to running out of water, that person will be penalized 10pt and the person with the most water from that group must hide in the partial square they are standing on or the nearest suitable partial square. If no one is left, that group loses).
  • The seekers who pass through the partial square with water and take the water will have their water bar filled fully (1 turn to take water per day), and the contestants can transfer water to each other.
  • Every day, the seeker has 2 turns to detect the status of the other team's seekers (where they are). Detecting is just able to used from 7am to 10pm in real time (outside of that time, this action would be unavailable).
  • Hider's Gambit: The Hider can choose to activate this powerful, high-risk ability once per exam.
    • Cost (The Gambit): Upon activation, the Hider's exact Main Square coordinate (e.g., e4) is immediately revealed to all contestants in a public notification.
    • Reward (The Payout): In exchange for this sacrifice, all living Seekers on the Hider's team will instantly receive +1 Search Turn and +1 Gather Turn. This ability can only be used if the Hider has not already used it.

üí°Definitions:

  • Coordinate: There are 3 types of coordinate:
    • Main squares: A square is directly defined by row and column (similar to a chessboard). It is one of the 100 basic tiles that make up the map‚Äôs 10√ó10 grid. The rows are numbered from 1 to 10, and the columns are labeled from A to J. A square is read by combining the lowercase column letter with the corresponding row number (for example: ‚Äòe4‚Äô, ‚Äòf5‚Äô, ‚Äòj10‚Äô).
    • Partial squares: One of the four quadrants that subdivide a single main square. On the 10x10 map grid, while a main square is identified by its coordinates (e.g., 'e4'), its four partial squares are identified with a numerical prefix (e.g., '1e4', '2e4', '3e4', and '4e4').
    • Super squares: A region composed of 9 main squares. It is named after its central main square (using a capital letter), with the other 8 main squares surrounding it. For example, the E4 super square consists of the e4 main square as its center, surrounded by d3, d4, d5, e3, e5, f3, f4, and f5. If a super square does not contain the full set of 9 main squares, the part of it that lies within the map is still defined as described above.
